  • Ian asked me a question out of Genesis after his swimming: “why is the moon walking?” I tried to explained it like this

The Moon appears to follow you because it is very far away, and objects like trees and houses that you pass by are very close by comparison. As you walk or drive along, things much closer to you, like trees and houses, appear to move between you and the Moon. Because the Moon is so far away, it doesn't seem to move at all (compared to you) and appears to be following you.

But obviously, he didn’t buy it: ” the moon is walking even when we stop walking!” So I had to say “let’s figure it out later.” Two days later, when I got out of house at night, I saw the moon is walking. Suddenly, i realized that because the clouds are moving instead of the moon.
