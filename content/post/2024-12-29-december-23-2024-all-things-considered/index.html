Dickens would walk the streets of London for hours at night, thinking.

The first edition sold out almost immediately.

It was an instant success, a kind of overnight success.

In England and around the world. And so in 1867, Dickens made a trip to the U.S. He traveled by train from as far south as Washington, D.C., and as far north as Maine, hosting hundreds of readings, hitting up major cities like New York and Boston.

“Christmas Carol” became kind of the do-it-yourself manual for how to do Christmas.
