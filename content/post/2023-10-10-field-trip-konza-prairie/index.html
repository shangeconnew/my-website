After the trip, I walked to Bergman to pick him up; but I was late for about 15 minutes. Ian cannot wait in Bergan and we met at the Hudsion trail. I was glad that he did not just wait there-He knew to walk home himself. It seemed like that you could make him decision.

On the way home, Ian shared with me the interesting stuff during the trip. They hiked about 6 miles; there is a vistor center and tour guide who he used a special name which I cannot remember now. also he fell to the trail either because of tumbling from one of his classmates or tripled by something else. He learned two things from the trip:

    1. He saw many bison there. Before, he shared with me a story about US buffaloes: because of the over hunting by the settlers, the population of bison was decreasing sharply and almost distinctive. It’s was president Roosevelt who saved them. Ian also told me that the difference between buffaloes and bison is the location: animals living in the North is called buffaloes and bison in the South. But the tour guide told them what they saw is bison.
    1. The tour guide told them that the fruits from rose are edible. He even asked me to guess what the name of the rose fruits is. I said “rose berry” and he said I’m right. It’s very interesting: we found the rose berry in the rose in the front yard of our Korean Neighbor. It turns out that I eat that fruit when I was a little Child.
