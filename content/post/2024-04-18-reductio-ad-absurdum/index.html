In English, it is used to refer to a method of argumentation where one disproves a proposition by showing that its logical conclusion leads to absurd or contradictory results. This approach is commonly used in logic and debate to demonstrate the invalidity of an argument.

Moreover, I learned:

"反问句" is indeed a term in Chinese. It translates to "rhetorical question" in English. In Chinese grammar and rhetoric, 反问句 (fǎn wèn jù) is a type of question used to express an idea indirectly, prompt reflection, or emphasize a point, much like in English. It serves similar purposes in communication and writing as rhetorical questions do in English.
  • “逆否命题”
(ní fǒu mìng tí) in Chinese refers to "contrapositive" in English. In logic, the contrapositive of a statement is formed by negating both the subject and predicate and reversing their order. It is an important concept in mathematical and logical reasoning, often used in proofs and argumentation.
